# Plateau Detection & Breakthrough Strategies

## What is a Plateau?

A plateau is when running performance stalls for 4+ weeks despite consistent training. Common signs:
- Same pace for the same effort on your key workouts
- No improvement in 5K/10K race times for 4+ weeks
- HR at easy pace not dropping over time
- Long run pace stagnant week to week

## Common Causes

1. **Same stimulus** — Doing the same workouts at the same pace week after week
2. **Too much hard running** — No easy days to recover (runners run everything at medium effort)
3. **Insufficient volume** — Not enough total weekly mileage to drive adaptation
4. **Missing key workout types** — No speed work, no long run, no tempo
5. **Accumulated fatigue** — Not taking deload weeks, chronic low-level fatigue

## Breakthrough Strategies

### 1. Change the Stimulus
- Swap one easy run for strides (4-6x 100m at mile pace)
- Replace a tempo run with a track interval session
- Add hill repeats every other week

### 2. Add Variation (Periodization)
```
Week 1-3: Build volume (increase weekly km 5-10%)
Week 4:    Deload (reduce volume 40-50%, keep intensity low)
Week 5-7:  Add intensity (tempo, intervals, or hills)
Week 8:    Race week or mini-taper
```

### 3. Check Your Easy Pace
If all your runs are at medium effort, your easy days aren't easy enough.
- Easy runs: Zone 2, conversational pace
- If you can't hold a conversation, slow down

### 4. Strength & Cross-Training
- Add 2 strength sessions per week (see cross-training/strength-for-runners.md)
- Consider cycling or swimming for active recovery
- Mobility work addresses running form inefficiencies

### 5. Review Nutrition & Recovery
- Are you eating enough to support your training volume?
- Sleep quality directly impacts performance
- Stress management matters — cortisol impairs recovery

## When to Consider a Program Change

| Runner Level | Plateau Duration | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Beginner | 4 weeks | Add 1 more run per week or increase long run |
| Intermediate | 6 weeks | Change workout mix: add interval block |
| Advanced | 8 weeks | Full program reset, new race goal, coach review |

## Rules
- Beginners plateau when they do the same thing too long — just adding volume often breaks it
- Intermediates need workout variety to break through
- Advanced runners may need periodization or a new race distance
- Use RPE consistently: if RPE drops for same pace, you're fitter (good)
- If RPE stays high for easy pace, you're fatigued (deload needed first)
